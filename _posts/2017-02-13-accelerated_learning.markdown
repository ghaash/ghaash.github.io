Learning is a magical thing, we humans have a great knack for it. We have come out of the caves to building machines that work for us using computer code. It is incredible.

Learning is also very hard.

Especially, when you are trying to learn something quickly, like coding.

The first time, I tried to learn something as quick as I could was learning Japanese. I went to Japan for a year and managed to gain fluency within six months. It was really hard. I was studying, speaking and reading as much as I could. I'd come home every day with my brain being mush, like when you study too hard.

Itt paid off, big time! You could drop me anywhere in Japan I could find my own around without any help. 

It also gave me a template on how learn things quickly. The best thing that I ever had done for myself.

I learned:

1. Put in as much time as you can into the thing you learn. --> So I quit my job for Flatiron School so I could pursue learning like a full-time job.

2. You are going to get mushy brain. --> So get as much sleep as you can spare. It also allows that big ol' brain of yours to understand things better.

3. You are going to get frsutrated. It's part of the learning process! --> So take breaks! Lots of them. And do fun things to take your mind off what you are learning and reduce brain mush. Otherwise you'll just burn out.

4. Make friends who are doing the same thing you are! --> You are the average of the five people you most spend time with. So do it with other coders!

4. Keep your eye on the finish line. --> Do not forget why you started this program! My first blog is all about this.
