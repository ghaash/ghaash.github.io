HTML and I go way back, twenty years back, to 1997. That's when I finally convinced my parents that the internet was the next big, we got the interwebz through a dial-up Earthlink account and it was glorious.

That alone spurred my interested in HTML and programming. How were web pages made? What were they made of? How did they talk to each other? How did my e-mail work? How did my video games work!?

I did some research, suprisingly on the internet and in books and discovered the world of programming. I was only 13 at the time and anything with a C (the C group of programming languages was too complex for me) was out of the question. 

So, I started with HTML. I learned it by going to web pages I visited it often, right-clicked on view source which spilled the bills on all of that glorious HTML. I'd take snippets from here and there, fiddle with them in notepad and upload them through a FTP to see what the changes were.

It was a lot of fun, you could just take people's code, modify it to your liking and have your own web page.

Those were the days, when the internet was new, and lawless.

I had a lot of fun.

edit:

Now that I've started the Flatiron School, I'm glad to see that HTML is all-new. I honestly still thought the web was just a very complex set of HTML. Glad to see that CSS, ruby and javascript are now a part of what makes the interwebz AWESOME!
