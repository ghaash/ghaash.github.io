I started coding for many reasons:

1. I am bored of customer service and retail positions, the face to face customer interaction wasn't just for me.

For the majority of life, most of my jobs have been positions in customer service. I was a lifeguard, an English Teacher, an officer in the U.S. Army, an adjuster for an insurance company, a personal trainer and a dog trainer. All of these jobs were front end customer service and the introvert in me just didn't like it. It wasn't a good fit, so I searched for something that was less like coding!

2. I wanted a skillset that would last me a lifetime.

I like learning, I like being able to do new things. Not all of those things can provide for me like coding can.

3. I'm very much so into technology particularly automation.

I'm so excited about Uber and its autonomous cars. I literally can't stop talking about it. It's literally going change the face of the earth and I would love to be part of it!

My predications for autonomous vehicles:
1. all cars will be autonomous in 5-10 years.
2. Freeways will actually work because a computer controlled vehicle can operate at high speeds safely!
3. Parking lots? Gone.
4. Insurance companies? gone.
5. The convenience of life will skyrocket due to ease of transportation.

4. I like it!

It's ironic, in college, my first major was computer science. Didn't like it one bit. It was droll. Boring. Professor stopped. Didn't see any opportunity to make a career, let alone any money. I tried a few of the flatiron programs and boom I was hooked.

5. I am tired of being poor.

I honestly don't make a lot of money and I'm tired of it. Coding is a good job and career. I want to be able to provide for my puppy. Whatever he wants.
